FDA Recall Classification Guide

Class I

Serious / Death Risk: A situation where there is a reasonable probability that use of the product will cause serious adverse health consequences or death. Example: NDMA contamination in valsartan batches.

Class II

Temporary Harm Risk: A situation where use of a product may cause temporary adverse health consequences or a remote probability of serious harm. Example: Super-potent tablet causing severe hypotension.

Class III

Unlikely Any Harm: A situation where use of a product is not likely to cause any adverse health consequence. Example: Labeling error involving only the lot number.
